Before Gambit

Since a young age I have always been fascinated with snakes

I remember a particular story from my childhood that really emphsises this. I grew up in Cyprus, a country that is hot and dry, perfect for reptiles. On one particlarly hot summer's day, my younger brother and I were playing outside. We always kept cats and as you may or may not know, cats, especially Cypriot ones, are really good at catching snakes. Well, on this day, one of our cats, Tommy, decided to catch a snake and bring it into our yard. Alive. I saw the snake and I was ecstatic. I ran to my mum to tell her and she responded in a way that was shocking to me. She reacted in pure fear. She closed us inside the house and ran to call the neighbour. I didn't know why but I didnt stay ignorant for long when I saw the neighbour run over with a shovel. I screamed and cried but it did nothing. The snake was now dead.

My mum said I didn't speak to her for days after the incident and I kept repeating how much I hated her.

She says that its from this point that she knew how attached I was to snakes. I started reading about them, learning about them, talking about them, and my family started learning more as a result and was exposed to the ignorance that surrounds snakes and the unbased fear of them.

I'm happy to say that my mum never caused the death of a snake again. In fact now she happily cares for one.

Getting Gambit

When I became a teenager, I realsied I could keep a snake as a pet. We already had many pets by this point, thanks to yours truly, so keeping pets was nothing new to my parents

However, this is where my mum would draw the line. Absolutely no snakes.

As is evidenced by this page, that stance did not last, but it was a tough one to break.

My dad and I launched a campaign. Every pet shop we went to we looked at the snakes. And every time we came back home we spoke about it with my mum.

This went on for a couple of years until one fateful day when I found Gambit. I still remmber when I picked him up from his plastic box in the pet shop how cold and small he was (the evils of the improper care often taken in the live pet industry that I was too young to understand). We rushed home, I begged my mum, my dad spoke to her, and by some miracle, she said a very reserved "yes".

I picked him up the next day and as they say, the rest is history.
